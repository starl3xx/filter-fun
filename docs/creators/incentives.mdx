# Creator Incentives

When you launch a token on filter.fun you sign up for a fixed economic model. This page explains the
three streams that flow to the token's creator address — and, importantly, when each one stops.

## The 0.20% trading-fee stream

Every trade on your pool pays a 2% protocol fee. **0.20% of that** routes to the creator
fee distributor and accrues to your address. No claim window, no week limit, no minimum balance.

You pull accrued fees by signing a `claim(token)` transaction from the address that launched the
token. The funds land at the creator-recipient address (defaults to your launching wallet; you can
delegate to a multisig or splitter via the admin console at `/token/<address>/admin`).

## When fees stop accruing

This is the section that matters most. Per spec §10.3 (locked 2026-05-02, Epic 1.16) the answer
depends entirely on what happens to your token's pool — there is no time cap and no settlement
cap in the contract:

- **For winners (the token wins its season):** fees accrue **forever**. Your pool keeps trading
  past settlement, and every trade keeps paying you 0.20%. There is no day-N cap, no
  settlement cap, and no maximum payout per token. A year out, ten years out, you keep earning
  if your pool keeps trading.

- **For filtered tokens:** fees stop because the pool's LP is unwound at the cut. No LP
  means no trades, no trades means no fees. The cap is implicit in the pool lifecycle, not
  enforced in code. Anything you accrued before the cut stays claimable forever.

- **For non-winning finalists:** same as filtered. The LP is unwound at final settlement
  (h168). Anything you earned across the week stays claimable; no new accrual after that.

The only way the contract redirects your fees away from you is if the deployer multisig invokes
the emergency-disable path (`disableCreatorFee`) — reserved for sanctioned or compromised
recipient addresses. This is a public on-chain action emitting `CreatorFeeDisabled` and is not
part of the normal lifecycle.

## The 2.5% champion bounty (winners only)

If your token wins its season, you also receive 2.5% of the losers pot at the moment the winner
is committed. This is a one-shot transfer at settlement, separate from the trading-fee stream.
Spec §10.4.

## Post-settlement routing — what changes

For winning pools, the fee split flips at settlement (spec §9.4). Pre-settlement the WETH-leg
breaks down as:

| Slice | BPS | Destination |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Prize pool | 90 | Season vault |
| Treasury | 65 | Treasury |
| Mechanics | 25 | Mechanics |
| **Creator** | **20** | **You** |

Post-settlement (after the winner is committed):

| Slice | BPS | Destination |
| --- | --- | --- |
| POL | 90 | POLVault (compounds your pool's backing) |
| Treasury | 65 | Treasury |
| Mechanics | 25 | Mechanics |
| **Creator** | **20** | **You** |

Same 0.20% to you in both regimes. The slice that used to feed the prize pool now compounds
into permanent backing for your pool — see [`/protocol/post-settlement-fees`](../protocol/post-settlement-fees)
for the rationale.

## Practical implications

- **The cost calculator on `/launch` shows a "Perpetual long-tail" number when you select
  the "wins" outcome.** That projection sums 12 weeks of decaying post-settlement volume at the
  current 0.20% creator slice. Over multi-month horizons this is the dominant ROI term — bigger
  than the launch-week revenue or the champion bounty.

- **Your admin console (`/token/<address>/admin`) shows lifetime accrual and a "still earning"
  badge for every token you've ever launched** that hasn't been emergency-disabled. The Claim
  button works for past tokens too — there's no "this week" filter.

- **Indexer transparency:** the `/tokens/<address>/creator-earnings` endpoint exposes
  `lifetimeAccrued`, `claimed`, `claimable`, `lastClaimAt`, and the multisig-disabled flag for
  any token. This is what the admin console reads; downstream tools (splitters, dashboards,
  taxes) can hit it directly.

## What didn't change

- Total trading fee = 200 bps = 2% of swap volume (unchanged across the boundary).
- Your slice = 20 bps = 0.20% (unchanged).
- The 2.5% champion bounty mechanic (§10.4) — still paid one-shot at settlement, still separate
  from the trading-fee stream.
